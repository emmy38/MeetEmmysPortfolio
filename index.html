About

Emmy grew up as a competitive tennis player until college where she studied Psychology. She discovered permaculture and ecovillages while living in San Francisco. After graduating and becoming a mother she tended her permaculture garden and her young son, and then became a Montessori Teacher for 5 years. She relocated to Breckenridge, Colorado to pursue her and her son's love of alpine sports.

Emmy spends her free time studying coding, practicing primitive skills in her sitspot on Peak 7, and climbs and swims at the gym with her son. Her goal is to connect landowners to skilled migrant and permanent ecovillagers through a network interface in order to create a tangible, resilient communities that exchange value through regenerative products.

Sustainability

In today's rapidly changing climate, the solution has been simplified to a network of ecovillages and homesteads that will work together in the worst case scenario of ecological disaster. Building strong bridges between these communities assists in coordinating growing a diverse range of foods and swapping overflow products to enjoy a larger variety of produce. Some homesteads do not have the resources to grow or harvest rice, grains, and nuts. Therefore, creating these networks is paramount to being able to comfortably live with the modern cuisines we have come to enjoy. Pooling our resources is paramount for resilience!

Using the Global Ecovillage Network Emmy is meeting with landtenders across the North American hemisphere to create personal connections while assisting in building a thriving virtual and interpersonal community.

  • Client: Return of the Village
  • Date: August 2010
  • Service: Board of Directors
